Parenting, Culture, and the Tiger Mother

January
Amy Chua

What does it take to raise successful, resilient children in an increasingly competitive world? Few people have sparked a more passionate global debate on that question than Amy Chua. A professor at Yale Law School and internationally bestselling author, Chua challenges conventional Western parenting assumptions and invites audiences to reconsider what it truly means to prepare a child for life. Her 2011 memoir Battle Hymn of the Tiger Mother became a runaway international bestseller, translated into over 30 languages, and ignited a worldwide conversation about Eastern and Western approaches to child-rearing and achievement.

Amy Chua is the John M. Duff Jr. Professor of Law at Yale Law School, where she has taught since 2001. She holds a J.D. from Harvard Law School (cum laude) and an A.B. from Harvard College (magna cum laude), where she was a Phi Beta Kappa scholar and John Harvard Scholar. She served as executive editor of the Harvard Law Review — the first Asian American to hold that post. Before joining the Yale faculty, she clerked for Chief Judge Patricia M. Wald on the U.S. Court of Appeals for the D.C. Circuit and practiced international law at Cleary, Gottlieb, Steen & Hamilton.

Professor Chua is a widely recognized expert in foreign policy, political tribalism, and ethnic conflict. Her book World on Fire: How Exporting Free Market Democracy Breeds Ethnic Hatred and Global Instability was a New York Times bestseller and named a Best Book of 2003 by both The Economist and The Guardian. Her subsequent work Political Tribes: Group Instinct and the Fate of Nations further cemented her reputation as one of the most provocative and insightful public intellectuals writing today.

In 2011, Time magazine named Amy Chua one of the 100 most influential people in the world. She has appeared on Good Morning America, The Today Show, The Colbert Report, and Real Time with Bill Maher, and has spoken at the Brookings Institution, the Aspen Ideas Festival, the World Economic Forum in Davos, and the CIA. She is a multiple-time recipient of Yale Law School's "Best Teaching" award.
